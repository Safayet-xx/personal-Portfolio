Automated Invoice maker and management System

This project is an Invoice Management Application that allows users to create, view, update, print, and manage invoices. It integrates Firebase Firestore as the backend for data storage and Cloudinary for image uploads. The application is built using React.js, with a responsive design that ensures a seamless user experience on both desktop and mobile devices. The project includes role-based features, such as restricting access to dashboard pages unless the user's profile is saved. It has a modular structure with reusable components for profile management, invoice handling, and dashboard navigation. The app is designed for businesses to manage customer details, product inventories, and invoice creation efficiently.

Detection and Classification of Diabetic Retinopathy Using GAN

Diabetic retinopathy (DR), a leading cause of vision loss, is a diabetes-related complication caused by retinal blood vessel damage. Its early detection and classification into stages (mild, moderate, severe, proliferative) are essential for timely treatment. My project focused on building a robust system for the detection and classification of DR using a combination of deep learning models and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs). We initially sought a dataset from Vision Eye Hospital, consulting with Prof. Dr. Siddiqur Rahman and senior doctors. Despite facing issues obtaining the dataset, but things didnâ€™t go on our way So, we had to utilized publicly available datasets such as Kaggle's APTOS 2019 or EyePACS for retinal images. Preprocessing steps included resizing images, enhancing contrast, and removing noise. A CNN-based architecture was developed for feature extraction and classification of DR stages, leveraging transfer learning to boost accuracy. To overcome dataset limitations, GANs were employed to generate synthetic retinal images, enhancing the diversity and balance of the training set. This integration of GANs and CNNs significantly improved the model's ability to generalize across varying DR features, achieving high accuracy and reliability in detection and classification. The project demonstrated the potential of AI in advancing automated DR diagnosis, particularly in resource-constrained settings, while laying the foundation for future improvements and research.
